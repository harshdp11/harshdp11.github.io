Adobe

About the Project

‘Moments of Delight in Documents’ was a 6-month internship project with the Document Cloud design team in Noida where I explored and prototyped ideas that can make reading pdfs more fun. The goal of the project was to focus on details and come up with interactions, micro-interactions, and animations that can lead to delightful user experiences.

Delight in User Experience

Aarron Walter from Mailchimp writes about the hierarchy of User Needs in his book Designing for Emotion. It’s similar to Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs, but rather than describing our physiological, safety, love and belonging, esteem, and self-actualization needs, he describes our needs as users. He says that at their foundation, our digital products must be functional—they have to work to solve a problem. They also need to be reliable—they need to be up and running at all times. And they need to be usable—easy to learn, easy to use, and easy to remember. Finally, they need to be pleasurable. This is where delight sits. In a way, we’re not fulfilling all of the user's needs if we don’t offer them a delightful product. This was a crucial learning which made me realize that delight is something worth exploring and sharing

Approach

1. Understand concept of Delight and micro-interactions and outline scope for the project.
2. Identify opportunity areas for delight and come up with ideas using journey maps
3. Prototype ideas(from step 2) and share them with the design team
4. Refining the most promising ones and clearly define the context in which they can be used

Outcome

During the course of the project I was able to come up with ideas which help in following use cases:
1. Navigating to a section in a document
2. Transitions between states and animations
4. Collaboration - Micro-interactions and animations for a collaborative document
5. Document changes - Interactions that help the user know about the document changes since their previous session
6. Discovery of related content- Helping the user in accessing critical/related information
